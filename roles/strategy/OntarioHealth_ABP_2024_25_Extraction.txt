STRATEGIC DIRECTION 1: REDUCE HEALTH INEQUITIES
=====================================

L1.1_TITLE: Reduce Health Inequities
L1.1_TEXT:
Through discrete strategies and across all areas of work, Ontario Health focuses on reducing
health inequities and dismantling barriers to improve access and delivery of care. The
overarching goals are to build capacity to understand and address discrimination and racism
in the system; grow and promote expertise in providing culturally responsive care; expand and
scale community-based health initiatives for underserved populations; and support emerging
areas of health inequities across Ontario aligned with provincial priorities.

---

L2.1.1_TITLE: Improve Equitable Outcomes and Experience for Equity-Deserving Communities
L2.1.1_TEXT:
Ontario Health is committed to improving equitable health care by reducing health inequities
and dismantling barriers to improve access and delivery of care. Work is organized around
specific population groups: First Nations, Inuit, Métis and Urban Indigenous populations;
Black communities; Francophone population; 2SLGBTQIA+ communities; refugee and asylum-seeking
and housing-insecure populations; people living with disabilities; and communities with
geographic disparities in access to care.
KEY_MEASURES:
- Number of initiatives with an aim to provide equitable access, experience, and health equity
  outcomes, with specific efforts made to address racism in the health sector experienced by
  equity-deserving populations.
- Number of signed Relationship Agreements with First Nation, Inuit, Métis and Urban Indigenous
  partners and Ontario Health.

---

L2.1.2_TITLE: Improve Access to Supportive Care in Housing
L2.1.2_TEXT:
Regional teams across the province work with delivery partners to support those requiring
alternate level of care to the best level of care and most appropriate care settings. This
includes expanding access and flow to community supports (assisted living services, supportive
housing, adult day programs, respite supports) and long-term care across the province, with
a focus on people living with disabilities and those who are vulnerably housed.
KEY_MEASURES:
- Increase capacity in assisted living services.

---

L2.1.3_TITLE: Advance Whole Person Care Experiences and Outcomes
L2.1.3_TEXT:
As part of commitment to the Quintuple Aim, activities include a focus on improved health
system navigation and the collection and use of holistic outcome and experience measures.
This includes enabling whole person care through comprehensive relationship-based primary care;
improving people's ability to navigate the health system; implementing provincially coordinated
patient-reported outcome and experience measurements (PROMs/PREMs); and enhancing Health811
to enable streamlined information access, health system navigation, and secure health data viewing.
KEY_MEASURES:
- nf (cross-referenced to sections 2.1, 2.6, and 2.8)

---

=====================================
STRATEGIC DIRECTION 2: ADVANCE THE RIGHT CARE IN THE RIGHT PLACE THROUGH
TRANSFORMING CARE WITH THE PERSON AT THE CENTRE
=====================================

L1.2_TITLE: Transform Care With the Person at the Centre
L1.2_TEXT:
Ontario Health applies a person-centred approach to advancing transformation initiatives of
the ministries. Focus areas will fundamentally advance health system integration and care for
patients and care partners across primary health care, mental health and addictions, home and
community care, aging care, access and flow, Ontario Health Teams, chronic disease, and
digital services.

---

L2.2.1_TITLE: Primary Health Care
L2.2.1_TEXT:
A key focus is to improve access to comprehensive team-based primary care for more of the
population. Ontario Health will support the expansion of interprofessional primary care teams
with a focus on equity-deserving populations and communities. Work includes supporting Primary
Care Networks within Ontario Health Teams (OHTs) and leveraging data-driven frameworks for
planning, performance measurement and quality improvement.
KEY_MEASURES:
- Number of new or expanding interprofessional primary care teams funded with new investments.
- Number of new people with access to team-based primary care.

---

L2.2.2_TITLE: Mental Health and Addictions Care
L2.2.2_TEXT:
The Mental Health and Addictions (MHA) Centre of Excellence supports Ontario in building a
comprehensive and connected mental health and addictions system. It plays a critical role in
overseeing the delivery and quality of MHA services and supports, including system management,
supporting quality improvement, disseminating evidence and setting service expectations. The
aim is to enhance access to, and quality of, mental health and addictions services and supports
for people living in Ontario.
KEY_MEASURES:
- Number of people accessing Ontario Structured Psychotherapy (OSP).
- Percent of clients with reliable improvement through the OSP program.

---

L2.2.3_TITLE: Home and Community Care
L2.2.3_TEXT:
To better support people in the community and realize the vision for a modernized and integrated
home and community care sector, Ontario Health will execute in three key areas: supporting the
integration of home and community care with the rest of the health system; enabling innovation
through home care service provider organizations, health service providers and OHTs; and advising
on enhanced Ministry investments. Actions will better connect home and community care with other
elements of the health care system, implement new models of care coordination and delivery, and
deploy new resources to improve equity, quality and access.
KEY_MEASURES:
- More home care access.
- Lower wait times to care.

---

L2.2.4_TITLE: Aging Care Continuum, Including Long-Term Care
L2.2.4_TEXT:
Ontario Health is focused on enhancing the experience of care and quality of life of all older
Ontarians. Working in collaboration with the Ministries of Health and Long-Term Care, health
service providers and system partners, a population health approach will be taken to implementing
an aging care continuum that aligns a comprehensive range of programs and services to support
older adults living in the community and in long-term care homes.
KEY_MEASURES:
- Less avoidable ED visits from long-term care residents.

---

L2.2.5_TITLE: Access and Flow
L2.2.5_TEXT:
The focus on enhancing system access and flow aims to improve patient outcomes and streamline
services across Ontario's health care spectrum, spanning hospital, supports in housing, home
and community care, long-term care and primary care. Ontario Health is dedicated to optimizing
access by ensuring quality care, allocating appropriate resources and fostering care integration
among health providers. This approach emphasizes delivering more community-based care to decrease
unnecessary ED visits and hospitalizations while minimizing hospital stays while waiting for
alternative levels of care in more appropriate care settings.
KEY_MEASURES:
- Lower wait times for care in more appropriate settings (lower alternate level of care length of stay).

---

L2.2.6_TITLE: Ontario Health Teams (OHTs)
L2.2.6_TEXT:
OHTs provide an integrated approach to organizing and delivering care that is more connected
to patients in their local communities. Under OHTs, health care and community providers work
as one coordinated team regardless of where they provide care, using a population health
management approach to improve how Ontarians experience and access care. Ontario Health
supports OHTs to deliver high quality integrated care and plays a critical role in supporting
the development and maturity of OHTs, including clinical, patient-facing and structural and
digital OHT priorities.
KEY_MEASURES:
- Number of OHTs operating approved integrated clinical pathways.
- Number of OHTs that have launched a home care leading project and/or have Ontario
  Health-approved plans for home care.

---

L2.2.7_TITLE: Chronic Disease Care
L2.2.7_TEXT:
With Ontarians living longer than ever, reducing incidence and improving outcomes of chronic
disease are critical to improving quality of life and building a sustainable health care system.
Chronic disease programs, with an initial focus on diabetes, will improve equitable access to
preventive care, early detection and chronic disease management services including remote care
management. The Preventive Care Program aims to improve access to preventive care for
equity-deserving and underserved populations, including personalized and culturally responsive
health education, health coaching, preventive care planning and service navigation.
KEY_MEASURES:
- Number of sites that have implemented the preventive care program.
- Percentage of patients with diabetes with up-to-date A1C and retinopathy screening.

---

L2.2.8_TITLE: Ontarians' Access to Digital Information and Services
L2.2.8_TEXT:
Providing Ontarians with digital access to health information and services is a priority.
Ontario Health is focused on enhancing the availability and user experience of its products
and services, including Health811 as the digital front door to the health system, providing
secure access to provincial electronic health data and services, and enabling more convenient,
connected patient journeys.
KEY_MEASURES:
- Health811 user satisfaction.
- Health811 utilization.

=====================================
STRATEGIC DIRECTION 3 & 4: ADVANCING FASTER ACCESS TO CARE AND SUPPORTING HEALTH CARE
WORKERS THROUGH HEALTH SYSTEM OPERATIONAL MANAGEMENT, COORDINATION, PERFORMANCE
MEASUREMENT AND MANAGEMENT, AND INTEGRATION
=====================================

L1.3_TITLE: Advance Faster Access to Care and Support Health Care Workers
L1.3_TEXT:
Ontario Health will improve the experience of Ontarians in accessing time-sensitive care and
support the front-line workforce through health system operational management, coordination,
performance measurement and management, and integration across emergency care, diagnostics,
surgical care, digital tools, and data systems, as well as workforce training, recruitment,
retention, and integrated capacity planning.

---

L2.3.1_TITLE: Emergency Care and Surge Response
L2.3.1_TEXT:
Ontario Health has established a strong foundation of provincial and regional emergency
management and surge response, operationally embedded through a regional model of health
system coordination and management. This includes developing an integrated critical care system
and enhancing the role of an emergency services program that provides clinical leadership
spanning the full spectrum of emergency services, developing standards and guidance, transferring
and exchanging knowledge, implementing digital and virtual solutions, and measuring, monitoring
and reporting performance.
KEY_MEASURES:
- Wait times to ED physician initial assessment.
- Acute and critical care bed capacity throughout cyclical and seasonal surge periods.

---

L2.3.2_TITLE: Integration of Diagnostics and Surgical Care
L2.3.2_TEXT:
Ontario Health continues to work with the Ministry to implement strategies to ensure that
patients receive care during clinically recommended wait times for procedures, imaging and
surgery through maximizing capacity and innovation. This includes implementing partnerships
between large and small hospitals or community clinics to support CT and MRI scanning, cataract
surgeries and GI endoscopy, as well as working on strategies to increase access to procedures
in community settings and advancing a pediatric strategy for comprehensive, integrated pediatric
care.
KEY_MEASURES:
- Percentage of surgeries completed within clinically recommended wait times for pediatrics
  and adults.
- Volumes of CT and MRI scans for pediatrics and adults.

---

L2.3.3_TITLE: Provider Access to Digital Tools
L2.3.3_TEXT:
Actions to support clinicians include expanding and streamlining provider access to digital
tools to reduce reliance on analog models such as fax machines. The Ministry's Patients Before
Paperwork initiative aims to reduce administrative burden on providers, reduce delays in
information sharing, support clinician wellbeing and improve patient safety and privacy, all
while allowing more time with patients. Ontario Health advances this initiative as a digitally
enabled clinical priority.
KEY_MEASURES:
- Number of sites onboarded and accessing provincial clinical viewers.

---

L2.3.4_TITLE: Data System Integration, Standardization and Security
L2.3.4_TEXT:
Ontario Health is prioritizing work to digitally unite providers and existing data stores
containing billions of patient records critical to high-quality and timely care. This includes
completing the Provincial Electronic Health Record (EHR), advancing data standardization and
interoperability, and scaling provincial cyber and privacy supports. Underpinning these efforts
are critical provincial cyber security supports to ensure security across the health care system.
KEY_MEASURES:
- Avoided or reduced cybersecurity breaches.

---

L2.3.5_TITLE: Use of Data and Analytics Services
L2.3.5_TEXT:
Accessing data for analytics and insights that improve system coordination and care delivery is
core to the daily work of Ontario Health. Ontario Health is working with the Ministry to establish
a modernized data authority model, including alignment of secure information management practices
combining technology, people and processes. Actions include expanding insights generated to drive
improvements in equity, clinical and quality outcomes, timely access to care, health system
integration and provider performance.
KEY_MEASURES:
- New datasets added to the Analytics Data Hub and utilized for planning, informing quality and
  clinical improvements, improving timely access and flow, and maintaining oversight and
  performance management of the health system.

---

L2.4.1_TITLE: Workforce Training and Optimization
L2.4.1_TEXT:
Ontario Health will foster a system that adaptively responds to provider experience, resulting
in the presence of a supported health workforce that enables better person-centred experiences
and outcomes. This work includes ensuring supports are in place to build capacity, address
training gaps, disseminate leading health workforce practices, and provide a more seamless
process to transition learners into priority areas of the workforce.
KEY_MEASURES:
- Number of nurses retained and recruited through the Community Commitment Program for Nurses.
- Number of nurses supported through the ED training program.

---

L2.4.2_TITLE: Recruitment, Retention and Distribution
L2.4.2_TEXT:
Improving provider experience includes increasing overall health system capacity through
recruitment, retention and distribution initiatives. This includes integrating internationally
educated health professionals into the health system, supporting overall recruitment and
retention through incentive-based programs for nurses and personal support workers, and
addressing distribution through rural and Northern supports such as relocation grants.
KEY_MEASURES:
- Number of internationally educated health professionals integrated and recruited into the
  health care system.
- Number of days of physician coverage provided to communities in need.
- Number of PSWs hired in long-term care and home and community care through PSW Incentive Program.
- Reduce avoidable ED shift closures for all HHR-related reasons.

---

L2.4.3_TITLE: Integrated Capacity Planning
L2.4.3_TEXT:
Capacity planning is a critical component to support prioritizing existing and future resources,
identify system pressure points, and help ensure workforce planning meets future population needs.
Ontario Health will support Ministry leadership in integrated capacity planning through data,
analytics, and provincial program, regional, and local insights.
KEY_MEASURES:
- Finalization of an implementation framework for the Health System Integrated Capacity Plan
  in partnership with the Ministry.
- Comprehensive health workforce data in place across multiple sectors.

---

=====================================
STRATEGIC DIRECTION 5: ENHANCE CLINICAL CARE AND SERVICE EXCELLENCE
=====================================

L1.5_TITLE: Enhance Clinical Care and Service Excellence
L1.5_TEXT:
As a clinical and quality-focused provincial agency, Ontario Health pursues the delivery of
best-in-class care through the experience and well-established success of its clinical programs.
Clinical programs span genetics, cancer, renal care, organ and tissue donation and transplants,
cardiac, vascular and stroke care, palliative care, and laboratory medicine.

---

L2.5.1_TITLE: Expand Provincial Genetic Services
L2.5.1_TEXT:
Ontario Health's provincial genetics program is building a comprehensive and connected system
for clinical genetic services in Ontario. This will provide access to timely, high-quality
genetic services for the best possible health outcomes and ensure health care providers have
the tools and resources they need to provide effective and coordinated genetic services.
KEY_MEASURES:
- Number of cancer tests performed (comprehensive biomarker and hereditary).
- Number of rare and inherited genetic tests performed.

---

L2.5.2_TITLE: Improve Access and Quality in Cancer Care
L2.5.2_TEXT:
Ontario Health's vision is to be a leader in cancer care by continuing to improve the quality,
safety and accessibility of cancer services from prevention through to long-term follow-up and
end-of-life care. A focus on cancer screening, cancer care and survivorship programs aims to
reduce the number of cancer diagnoses and to improve patient outcomes and experience.
KEY_MEASURES:
- Cancer screening participation rates (colorectal, cervical, breast).
- Percent P3 CT scans and MRI scans complete within recommended wait time (adult and pediatric).

---

L2.5.3_TITLE: Improve Access and Quality in Renal Care
L2.5.3_TEXT:
Ontario Health is the government's advisor on chronic kidney disease and the renal care system,
and also funds, coordinates and provides clinical guidance on the delivery of services to people
with chronic kidney disease. Through collaborative efforts, Ontario Health is committed to
advancing a high-quality and person-centred system of care for Ontarians with chronic kidney
disease, with a focus on increasing kidney transplantation and home dialysis.
KEY_MEASURES:
- Increase the percentage of end-stage renal patients who received a living or deceased donor
  kidney transplant.
- Increase the percentage of chronic dialysis patients on a home dialysis modality.

---

L2.5.4_TITLE: Increase Life-Saving Organ and Tissue Donations and Transplants
L2.5.4_TEXT:
Ontario Health is responsible for delivering and coordinating organ and tissue donation and
transplantation services across the province. Ontario Health helps save and enhance lives by
maximizing organ and tissue donations in partnership with stakeholders. Public education and
awareness initiatives support efforts to increase donor registration rates.
KEY_MEASURES:
- Net increase in registered donors.
- Total living and deceased donor transplants.

---

L2.5.5_TITLE: Improve Access and Quality in Cardiac, Vascular, and Stroke Care
L2.5.5_TEXT:
Ontario Health provides clinical leadership to support priorities and initiatives that aim to
improve equitable access to and quality of cardiac, stroke and vascular care for all Ontarians.
The focus is on developing standards and guidance; defining evidence-based clinical pathways
and models of care; knowledge transfer and exchange; developing strategies to reduce variations
in services; assessing capacity and informing funding policy; and implementing performance
measurement, monitoring and reporting frameworks.
KEY_MEASURES:
- Reduce heart failure admissions per 100 heart failure patients.
- Increase endovascular Thrombectomy Treatment (EVT) for ischemic stroke.
- Reduce non-traumatic major lower limb amputation (per 100,000).

---

L2.5.6_TITLE: Transform and Improve Access and Quality in Palliative Care
L2.5.6_TEXT:
Ontario Health aims to increase timely and equitable access to safe, high-quality and effective
palliative care for people who are living with a serious illness and their care partners. A
palliative approach to care, alongside curative treatment, offers improved quality of life and
management of pain and other symptoms, including support to die in the individual's preferred
location of death.
KEY_MEASURES:
- Number of community organizations implementing the model of care recommendations for adults
  in the community.

---

L2.5.7_TITLE: Expand Ontario Laboratory Medicine Program
L2.5.7_TEXT:
The Ontario Laboratory Medicine Program (OLMP) will enable a unified provincial plan and
integrated approach to laboratory medicine across the health system that is patient-centred,
innovative and sustainable and delivers the greatest value for Ontarians. The OLMP will build
on clinical expertise of its clinical and scientific advisory groups; focus on equitable access
to testing alongside appropriate and coordinated tests and point-of-care testing; foster
innovation and system responsiveness; support digital integration; and enable high-quality
laboratory diagnostics for providers, patients and system surveillance.
KEY_MEASURES:
- End-to-end turnaround times of select provincial laboratory medicine and testing programs.

STRATEGIC DIRECTION 6: MAXIMIZE SYSTEM VALUE BY APPLYING EVIDENCE
=====================================

L1.6_TITLE: Maximize System Value by Applying Evidence
L1.6_TEXT:
Improving outcomes across emerging and high priority clinical areas requires a strong capacity
for health care improvement and supporting knowledge exchange. Ontario Health works with
patients, providers and organizations across the health system to improve outcomes, promote
health equity and patient safety, standardize care, and enhance patient and provider experience.

---

L2.6.1_TITLE: Advance High-Quality and Safe Care Through Evidence and Continuous Quality Improvement
L2.6.1_TEXT:
Ontario Health drives health system and patient care improvements through clinical and quality
standards that are reflective of Ministry and Ontario Health priorities. This includes developing
and disseminating quality standards, advancing clinical adoption of evidence-based care,
delivering Quality Improvement Plan guidance, facilitating continuous health care improvements
through clinical quality initiatives, and advancing health technology assessments to inform
public funding recommendations.
KEY_MEASURES:
- Rate of implementation and adoption of accepted health technology assessment recommendations.

---

L2.6.2_TITLE: Strengthen System Supports and Accountabilities
L2.6.2_TEXT:
Ontario Health continues to strengthen its partnerships with health system providers across the
province, supporting the sustainment of health care system operations through the issuance and
management of service accountability agreements with delivery partners that enforce system goals
and enable strong system accountability and performance management best practices. Ontario Health
also works closely with the Ministry in developing and implementing funding policy and innovative
funding models and managing case-costing programs, aimed at leveraging funding to drive value
and performance in the system.
KEY_MEASURES:
- nf

STRATEGIC DIRECTION 7: STRENGTHEN ONTARIO HEALTH'S ABILITY TO LEAD
=====================================

L1.7_TITLE: Strengthen Ontario Health's Ability to Lead
L1.7_TEXT:
Ontario Health is committed to engaging, informing and inspiring its workforce to enable them
to do their best work. The organization continuously invests in people through wellness programs
and learning and development programs that enable individual growth, and continuously embeds
Ontario Health's vision, mission and values into its work.

---

L2.7.1_TITLE: Enhance Ontario Health's Organizational Effectiveness Through a Strong, Engaged, Connected,
Diverse, and Accountable Workforce
L2.7.1_TEXT:
Ontario Health will invest in its people through wellness programs and learning and development
programs that enable individual growth, and will continuously embed Ontario Health's vision,
mission and values into work. This includes advancing a strong and engaged workforce through
employee engagement and action plans, advancing return to workplace strategy, implementing
communications strategy, and implementing organizational Equity, Inclusion, Diversity and
Anti-Racism commitments.
KEY_MEASURES:
- Workforce turnover.
- Workforce engagement and experience.

---

L2.7.2_TITLE: Support the Government's Plans for Supply Chain Centralization
L2.7.2_TEXT:
Ontario Health's supply chain delivers a critical role bringing vendor-based solutions and
services that drive improved health care outcomes in various key programs such as screening,
capital purchases for medical equipment and digitally enabled solutions. Working with key
stakeholders including the Ministry and Supply Ontario on procurement strategy alignment will
support enhanced value for money through best procurement contract outcomes.
KEY_MEASURES:
- Vendor performance is measured and monitored through key vendor contract management
  mechanisms (e.g., service levels) to better ensure service expectations are being met.

SPECIFICITY: High
SCOPE: Comprehensive
OVERALL_QUALITY_NOTES: Highly detailed provincial-level business plan with clear strategic architecture.
The document provides strong hierarchical structure with 7 strategic directions, 20 sub-priorities
(L2), and measurable Key Performance Indicators for each. Vision, Mission, and Values are explicitly
stated. The document is explicit about its accountability context - driven by the Ministry of Health's
"Your Health" plan and Letter of Direction, the Ministry of Long-Term Care strategic priorities
letter, and the Connected Care Act, 2019. Unlike individual hospital strategic plans, this document
operates at the system integration and oversight level, not direct service delivery. Many priorities
are framed as enabling or coordinating hospital and community sector performance rather than
delivering care directly. The document includes extensive operational, financial, and HR appendices
which have been excluded from this extraction as they are implementation rather than strategic
content. Key themes running across all directions include: health equity and reducing inequities;
digital transformation and data integration; Ontario Health Teams as the primary integration
mechanism; workforce stabilization and development; and person-centred care. This document serves
as the primary reference comparator for the Ontario Hospital concordance analysis project.
