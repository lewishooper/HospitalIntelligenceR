# Strategic Plan Status and Hospital Financial Trajectories in Ontario
## Technical Narrative — HIT Analytics Workstream
### HospitalIntelligenceR | May 2026

---

## Overview

This document describes the methodology used to produce Tables HIT-03a (Revenue) and HIT-03b (Expense): a comparison of field-adjusted cumulative financial trajectories between Ontario acute care hospitals with and without an active strategic plan in place. The tables are generated by `hit_03_plan_comparison.R` and formatted for publication by `fig_hit03_plan_tables.R`.

---

## 1. Data Sources

Three files feed this analysis, all treated as read-only inputs:

**`roles/hit/outputs/hit_master.csv`** — the primary HIT data file. Long format: one row per FAC × fiscal year × indicator code. Covers 149 unique FACs across 7 fiscal years (2018/2019 through 2024/2025) and 55 indicators. This file is the output of the HIT import pipeline and is never modified by analytical scripts.

**`analysis/data/hospital_spine.csv`** — the authoritative FAC scope reference. Defines the study population of Ontario acute care hospitals by MOH FAC identifier. HIT-only entities and retired pre-merger FAC codes present in `hit_master.csv` are excluded by filtering to spine FACs.

**`analysis/data/strategy_classified.csv`** — direction-level strategic plan data. One row per strategic direction per hospital. Contains `plan_period_start` (the plan's start year, stored as a Date type) and `plan_period_end` for each hospital's current strategic plan.

## 2. Scope and Exclusions

**Registry scope.** The spine defines 134 active acute care hospitals. Four structural outliers are excluded from all calculations:

| FAC | Hospital | Reason |
|-----|----------|--------|
| 854 | SA Grace Toronto | COVID-era transitional care expansion — atypical revenue/volume profile |
| 971 | Sudbury St. Joseph's Continuing Care | COVID-era transitional care expansion |
| 701 | Richmond Hill Mackenzie Health | Large capital construction onboarding — cost structure non-comparable |
| 938 | Haliburton Health Services (Dysart et al) | Amalgamation / MOHLTC review period |

These four FACs are excluded before field median calculations. Their removal is applied at the analytics layer; `hit_master.csv` is never modified. This leaves 130 FACs in the analytical scope.

**FAC 983 (Huron Perth Healthcare Alliance).** This merged entity appears in the spine and has strategy data but has no HIT data reported under the consolidated FAC code. It is excluded from the financial trajectory analysis and noted in the table footnote.

**Working indicators.** Six indicators are extracted from `hit_master.csv` for this analysis:

| Code | Label | Role |
|------|-------|------|
| `ind01` | Total Revenue | Primary — revenue trajectory |
| `ind02` | Total Expenses | Primary — expense trajectory |
| `ind05` | Total Margin, Hospital PSC 1 | Lead margin indicator |
| `ind06` | % Non-MOH Revenue | Used to derive MOH revenue |
| `ind45` | Total Acute Inpatient Days | Service volume denominator |
| `ind56` | Total Emergency Visits (Face-to-Face, In-House) | Secondary volume indicator |

One field is derived within the script: `moh_revenue = ind01 × (1 − ind06 / 100)`. No direct MOH revenue indicator exists in the HIT data.

## 3. Field Adjustment Methodology

Raw year-over-year (YoY) changes in hospital revenue and expenses reflect a combination of hospital-specific performance and sector-wide forces — particularly Ministry of Health (MOH) base funding transfers, which move all hospitals simultaneously. The COVID period (fiscal years 2020/2021 through 2022/2023) involved extraordinary sector-wide funding flows that would otherwise dominate any longitudinal comparison.

Field adjustment removes these sector-wide effects by subtracting the field median YoY change from each hospital's individual YoY change for that transition year:

> **Field-adjusted YoY change = Hospital YoY % change − Field median YoY % change**

Field medians are computed separately for revenue and expenses, across all 130 scope hospitals with non-missing values for that transition. A hospital at the field median in any given year receives a field-adjusted score of zero for that transition — it neither outperformed nor underperformed the sector.

The resulting field medians by transition year confirm that COVID-period anomalies are correctly captured and removed by this procedure:

| Transition Year | Field Median Rev YoY | Field Median Exp YoY | N FACs |
|-----------------|---------------------|---------------------|--------|
| 2019/2020 | +3.6pp | +3.9pp | 128 |
| 2020/2021 | +11.8pp | +9.2pp | 128 |
| 2021/2022 | +1.8pp | +4.3pp | 128 |
| 2022/2023 | +4.5pp | +8.0pp | 129 |
| 2023/2024 | +11.0pp | +9.5pp | 129 |
| 2024/2025 | +5.3pp | +6.3pp | 129 |

The 2020/2021 and 2023/2024 spikes reflect MOH emergency transfer flows and post-COVID reconciliation payments respectively.

Because the with-plan group shows left skew on both dimensions — the revenue mean (1.0pp) sits below the median (1.9pp), indicating a small number of hospitals with strongly negative adjusted trajectories pulling the mean down — the median is the more representative measure of the typical hospital's experience and is treated as the lead statistic in the tables. The mean is retained for completeness and to characterise the distributional tail.

**Cumulative field-adjusted change** is the sum of field-adjusted YoY changes across all transition years within a hospital's measurement window. A hospital with a cumulative revenue score of +5.0pp grew its revenue 5 percentage points more than the field median, in total, over its observation window.

## 4. Strategic Plan Classification

Strategic plan dates are drawn from `strategy_classified.csv`, which contains one row per strategic direction per hospital. The `plan_period_start` field records the calendar year in which each hospital's current strategic plan began.

**FAC-level collapse.** The direction-level file is collapsed to one row per FAC by taking the minimum `plan_period_start` across all directions for that hospital. In practice, all directions within a hospital's plan share the same start year, so this is a deduplication step.

**No-plan hospitals.** Hospitals present in the spine but absent from `strategy_classified.csv`, or present with all-NA `plan_period_start` values, are classified as having no strategic plan. Ten hospitals (7.7% of scope) fall into this group. These are hospitals for which no strategic plan document was located during the Phase 1 extraction; their absence from the plan data reflects access barriers (websites without public plan links, JavaScript-rendered download buttons without direct PDF URLs) rather than confirmed absence of a planning process.

**Fiscal year bridge rule.** Plan start year Y maps to first treatment fiscal year "Y/Y+1". The transition year "Y−1/Y" is excluded from the with-plan measurement window (conservative intent: the plan was adopted mid-year or after budget setting, so its first full influence is the following year).

---

## 5. Two-Year Tenure Filter

A hospital's plan is counted in the primary "with plan" comparison group only if at least two YoY transition observations are available within its measurement window. This ensures a minimum comparable observation period.

Of 120 hospitals with non-NA plan dates, the tenure filter produces the following classification:

| Group | N | Notes |
|-------|---|-------|
| With plan, meets ≥2-year tenure | 55 | Primary comparison group; 2015–2023 plan vintage |
| With plan, below tenure threshold | 65 | Primarily 2024–2026 plan vintage; excluded from primary table |
| No strategic plan | 10 | Full HIT window used as measurement period |

The 65 short-tenure hospitals are not absent from the sector — they represent the majority of Ontario hospitals currently operating under recently adopted plans. Their exclusion from the primary comparison is a methodological boundary, not an analytic judgment about their plans. A separate vintage cohort analysis (comparing trajectory by plan start year cohort) is the appropriate next step for this group.

---

## 6. Measurement Windows

The two comparison groups are measured over different time horizons by design:

**With plan group.** Each hospital's cumulative score is computed from its first treatment fiscal year (Y/Y+1) through the most recent available HIT year (2024/2025). The length of the observation window therefore varies by plan vintage: a 2019-vintage hospital has up to five YoY transitions in its window; a 2023-vintage hospital has two.

**No plan group.** Each hospital's cumulative score is computed over the full available HIT series (six YoY transitions: 2019/2020 through 2024/2025), including the COVID disruption years.

**This asymmetry is by design but must be stated explicitly.** Because the with-plan group's measurement windows are predominantly anchored in the post-2021 recovery period (most plans are 2022–2023 vintage), these hospitals avoid the worst COVID disruption transitions in their measurement window. This advantage is partially offset by the field adjustment, which removes the sector-wide recovery lift. However, hospital-specific timing effects within the recovery — faster or slower MOH transfer reconciliation — are not fully controlled. The footnote language in the published tables acknowledges this limitation.

---

## 7. Summary Statistics

Four statistics are reported per group in each table:

**N Hospitals** — count of hospitals in the group meeting the inclusion criteria. Hospitals with missing revenue or expense data in any transition year within their window contribute fewer observations to that year's YoY calculation but remain in the cumulative score (missing transitions contribute zero field-adjusted change for that year via `na.rm = TRUE` in the sum).

**Mean (pp)** — arithmetic mean of cumulative field-adjusted change across hospitals in the group. Rounded to one decimal place. Read alongside the median given the left skew in the with-plan group.

**Median (pp)** — median of cumulative field-adjusted change. Lead statistic; more robust to the hospital-level outliers pulling the mean below the typical hospital's experience.

**SD (pp)** — standard deviation of cumulative field-adjusted change. The no-plan group shows substantially higher SD on both revenue (15.8pp) and expense (9.8pp) compared to the with-plan group (6.7pp and 6.6pp respectively), reflecting the heterogeneity of a small and structurally diverse group. This variability is the primary reason the no-plan group results should be treated as directionally informative rather than precise.

## 8. Type Composition Check

Hospital type composition across groups (used to assess whether the observed gap is driven by differential representation of high-performing types):

| Type Group | With Plan (n=55) | No Plan (n=10) |
|------------|-----------------|----------------|
| Community — Large | 23 (41.8%) | 5 (50.0%) |
| Community — Small | 24 (43.6%) | 4 (40.0%) |
| Teaching | 5 (9.1%) | 0 (0.0%) |
| Specialty | 3 (5.5%) | 1 (10.0%) |

The no-plan group has modestly higher Community Large representation (50%) than the with-plan group (42%). Because Community Large hospitals showed the strongest trajectories in the type-stratified analysis (hit_02), this composition difference biases against the observed gap: if anything, the no-plan group should outperform given its type composition. The fact that it does not reinforces the directional finding, though not the causal interpretation.

---

## 9. Interpretive Boundaries

The tables are appropriately treated as descriptive baselines, not estimates of a plan effect. Three structural caveats apply:

1. **Measurement window asymmetry.** The with-plan group is predominantly measured during the post-COVID recovery window. The field adjustment removes the sector-wide recovery lift but not hospital-specific recovery timing variation.

2. **Selection into planning.** Hospitals without accessible strategic plans are a small and likely non-random group. Plan absence may be a symptom of the same structural pressures driving negative trajectories rather than a cause.

3. **No-plan group size.** With n=10 and SD=15.8pp on revenue, the no-plan group mean carries a 95% confidence interval spanning roughly −12pp to +8pp. It is directionally stable (mean and median are identical at −1.9pp) but statistically imprecise.

The more analytically controlled comparison — same hospitals, same measurement window, same structural constraints — is the FIN strategic theme analysis (hit_02), where hospitals with and without a Financial Sustainability direction showed near-identical trajectory distributions. That null finding is the stronger analytical result. The plan status comparison in Tables HIT-03a/b provides context for the sector-wide descriptive picture.
